CEW+

The Center for the Education of Women+ (CEW+) at the University of Michigan is a comprehensive resource that supports a diverse range of students, particularly focusing on women and other marginalized groups. CEW+ offers a variety of services aimed at promoting the academic, professional, and personal growth of students. These services include counseling, financial aid resources, and a host of educational programs designed to empower individuals to overcome barriers in their educational and career journeys.

One of the key services provided by CEW+ is its counseling program. This includes one-on-one counseling sessions on subjects such as career exploration, academic concerns, and work-life balance. Counselors help students identify their goals and develop actionable plans to achieve them. Additionally, CEW+ offers financial support through scholarships and fellowships, which can be particularly beneficial for students who may face financial hardships while pursuing their education.

Students can engage with CEW+ through a variety of channels. The center regularly hosts workshops, seminars, and events that tackle important issues like gender equity, leadership development, and career advancement. These events provide networking opportunities and foster a sense of community among participants. Moreover, students can access resources like the CEW+ Library, which houses a collection of materials on relevant topics. By engaging with CEW+, students can not only receive direct assistance but also connect with a supportive network that can bolster their academic and professional trajectories.
